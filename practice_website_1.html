The Color Green

Where the color green originated from:

The name for the color green originated from the Old English word "grene" which connects to the ideas of growth and grass. The color green has been used since the Ancient Egyptian times. They used something called malachite to make the color while Greeks used verdigris. The color green had a huge impact during the Impressionist movement and the emeral green color started growing its popuularity in fashion in the 1800s.

Fun Facts about the Color Green:

  • Green was depicted with an evil connotation during the Medieval times where the used green to depict the devil.
  • The color Scheele's Green was made in 1775 with tocic chemicals which, if exposed to for an extended amount of time, would lead to sickness or even death.
  • The wavelengths that allow the perception of the color green are between 510 and 570 nanometers
